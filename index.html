Babel Between Us

Babel Between Us is a literary experiment where 18 writers and 9 ethnographers from all over the world, over the course of two years, wrote collaboratively. The project resulted in a cartographic visualization of a collaboratively created world, exploring the uncharted waters between collaboration, fiction, and ethnography. Our aim was to explore if a collaborative writing process could be aided by a digital ethnography made on the writing as it happened. The writers wrote on a forum, the ethnographers analyzed the writing as they happened, and fed this back to the writers, creating a feedback loop between the creative output and the analysis of it. We wanted to know how this affected the collaborative process, if it made it easier to get a grasp of the direction of the group, and if it opened new interesting entry points for the creative process.

The analysis was done through ethnographic coding, which essentially is adding labels to parts of the text of reocurring meaning. These labels are then extracted and used to build a conceptual map of the fiction, by grouping together concepts that often co-occur. For example, the ethnographic code “boundlessness” has often been applied to gether with codes such as “darkness” and “emptiness”.

boundlessness

The absolvement or lack of boundaries. As in explicitly pointing out that the ocean is endless, or that a tower is so tall no one knows how many floors there are.

'boundlessness' has often been applied together with meticulous categorisation, darkness, emptiness, death, limits of one's consciousness, accepting death, the university, bottleneck, pandemic, indifference, self-reflection, the city, recurring dream, dream-like quality and staircase

The writing started in March 2020, just as the world went into lockdown. The writers contributed from their different corners of the world into what became a sort of remotely conducted Decamerone, weaving their real experiences of the pandemic with dreams of escapes to other worlds.

A series of viral events

As lockdowns succeeded one another we continued writing and analyzing, adding and removing rules of the game. The writers got the option to lock threads, to fork threads, and we arranged monthly video calls to discuss the evolution of the corpus. Fights occurred, friendships were formed, and a lot of interesting text was written.

We hope this project will inspire other similar explorations. All the technology developed is open source. If you are interested in doing something similar, please do reach out to us!

Babel Between Us is made by Ola Claësson, Jennifer McGeachie, Filip Nyström, Alja Gudžević, Lea Vene, Bojan Mucko, Hugi Ásgeirsson and Jakob Skote, with Amelia Hassoun as the senior ethnographer.
